## The Problem With Candy AI's Pricing Model

Let us be specific about how Candy AI's pricing works, because this is the core issue driving people to look for alternatives.

When you sign up for Candy AI, the base subscription starts from $5.99 per month. That subscription covers text chat with your AI companion. So far, so normal. But Candy AI's best feature — image generation — is not included. Generating images of your AI girlfriend requires tokens, sometimes called gems. These tokens cost real money on top of your subscription.

Here is where the math gets uncomfortable. A single image generation can cost anywhere from a handful of tokens to a significant chunk depending on the complexity and style. If you are actively generating images a few times per day, which is normal usage for a platform that markets itself on visual content, those tokens add up fast. We tracked our own spending during testing and found that our actual monthly cost was consistently $20-25, despite being on a plan advertised at $12.99 per month. Reddit threads confirm this is typical. Search "Candy AI cost" on any AI companion subreddit and you will find users reporting real monthly spending of $15-30.

Premium interactions — the kind of content that most users are actually there for — also draw from your token balance. So the subscription fee gets you in the door, and then you pay again for the things that make the platform worth using.

Compare this to how most competitors handle pricing. Platforms like [MyDreamGirlfriend.ai](/) and Nomi.ai charge a flat monthly rate that includes everything: chat, image generation, premium features, voice — all of it. There are no tokens, no gems, no secondary currency. You pay your monthly fee and every feature is available. The price you see is the price you pay.

The token system also creates a psychological problem that goes beyond the money itself. When every image request triggers a mental calculation about your remaining balance, it breaks immersion. You are supposed to be having an experience with an AI companion. Instead, you are doing cost-benefit analysis. That is the opposite of what these platforms should feel like.

## What to Look for in a Candy AI Alternative

Candy AI is not a bad product. Before we list alternatives, it is worth acknowledging what it does well so you know what you would be giving up — and what you should demand from a replacement.

**What Candy AI does well:**

- High-quality AI image generation — this is genuinely one of the best in the market
- A large, active community with thousands of premade characters
- Clean, polished interface that feels professional
- Regular feature updates and active development
- Uncensored content available from the start
- Live action mode for dynamic interactions

**What Candy AI lacks:**

- **Flat pricing** — the token system means your real cost is unpredictable and often much higher than advertised
- **Persistent memory** — limited recall of details from earlier conversations, especially beyond a few sessions
- **Relationship depth** — no structured progression system; the relationship feels static regardless of how long you chat
- **Deep personality customization** — characters feel similar under the surface despite different appearances and backstories
- **End-to-end encryption** — standard encryption only, which matters for intimate conversations
- **Voice features** — limited compared to competitors with dedicated voice systems

We evaluated every alternative below on these criteria: image quality, conversation depth, memory, customization, relationship mechanics, privacy, voice features, and — most importantly — pricing transparency. The goal is to find platforms that match or exceed Candy AI's strengths while fixing its biggest weakness.
