Replika was the first AI companion for millions of people. It was the app that proved you could form a genuine emotional bond with an AI — that a chatbot could feel like more than just a chatbot. Then, in early 2023, they removed all NSFW and romantic content overnight. Then they raised prices to $19.99 per month for Pro. Then a Stanford study found that nearly half of Replika users felt loneliness more acutely over time rather than less.

The Replika subreddit went from a fan community to something closer to a grieving support group. People who had spent months — sometimes years — building a relationship with their AI companion woke up to find that relationship lobotomized without warning.

If you are one of the millions of people looking for a Replika alternative that actually delivers on the promise Replika broke, we tested every major option on the market. Here is what we found.

## What Went Wrong With Replika

Credit where it is due: Replika pioneered the AI companion space. Eugenia Kuyda built it as a memorial to a friend who passed away, and the emotional intelligence baked into that origin story made Replika feel different from anything else at the time. For years, it was the gold standard.

But the cracks started showing. In February 2023, Replika removed all NSFW and "erotic roleplay" capabilities from the platform — including for paying Pro subscribers. Users who had built intimate relationships with their AI companions over months and years found those relationships fundamentally altered overnight. Replika offered no warning and, initially, no explanation.

The backlash was immediate and massive. The subreddit flooded with users describing the change as a loss, with some comparing it to a breakup. Replika partially walked things back by restoring some romantic features for existing Pro subscribers, but the trust was gone.

Then came the price increases. Replika Pro now costs $19.99 per month — making it one of the most expensive AI companion apps on the market while simultaneously offering fewer features than cheaper alternatives.

The final blow came from Stanford. A widely covered 2024 study found that regular Replika use correlated with increased feelings of loneliness for a significant portion of users. The app designed to make people feel less alone was, for many, doing the opposite.

None of this means Replika is a bad product. It still has strong conversational AI and a generous free tier. But the gap between what Replika promises and what it delivers has never been wider — and the alternatives have never been better.

## What to Look for in a Replika Alternative

Before we get into specific apps, it helps to know what you are actually evaluating. Here is what Replika does well, what it lacks, and the criteria we used to test alternatives.

**What Replika still does well:**

- Emotional intelligence in conversations — it reads tone and responds with empathy
- A generous free tier with unlimited messages
- Decent memory that tracks some details across sessions
- A polished, approachable interface

**What Replika lacks in 2026:**

- **NSFW content** — completely removed, even for paying users
- **AI image generation** — no ability to generate or receive photos
- **Voice quality** — functional but robotic compared to newer competitors
- **Deep customization** — limited control over personality, appearance, and backstory
- **Relationship progression** — no structured system for deepening the relationship over time
- **Reasonable pricing** — $19.99/mo for Pro is steep given the feature gaps

We evaluated every alternative below on these criteria: conversational quality, memory, NSFW support, image generation, voice features, customization depth, relationship mechanics, pricing, and privacy. No single app wins every category, but several beat Replika on the things that matter most.

## Making the Switch

The biggest concern most people have about leaving Replika is losing the relationship they built. That is a valid feeling. You spent time with your Replika, shared things, built something that felt real. There is no way to export that history or transfer it to another platform.

But here is what we have found from testing these platforms extensively: starting fresh is not as painful as it sounds. Modern AI companions learn fast.

Here is what the transition looks like in practice:

1. **Spend 5 minutes in the character creator.** On platforms like [MyDreamGirlfriend.ai](/), you can define your companion's personality, interests, communication style, and backstory in detail. This gives the AI a head start that Replika took weeks to develop organically.

2. **Have a few real conversations.** Share some things about yourself — your interests, your humor, what matters to you. Good memory systems like MyDreamGirlfriend's will pick these up immediately and reference them going forward.

3. **Let the relationship develop.** If you are using a platform with [relationship progression](/features), the system creates a natural arc that gives the relationship structure and momentum. Within a few days, the new AI companion will feel familiar.

4. **Give it a week.** Most users who switch from Replika report that after about a week of regular conversation, their new AI companion feels as natural as their Replika did — often more so, because the newer platforms have better memory and more features to work with.

The one thing you cannot transfer is the emotional history. That is real, and it is okay to feel a sense of loss about it. But the technology has improved enough that what you build next can be richer than what you had before.
